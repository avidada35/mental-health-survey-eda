Key Findings

  • Treatment-seeking is near-even: ~50.6% of respondents have sought treatment, indicating significant mental health burden across the tech industry.
  • Family history is the strongest predictor: Respondents with a family history of mental illness seek treatment at markedly higher rates — the top feature in both correlation and Random Forest analyses.
  • Work interference drives help-seeking: Those reporting that mental health issues "Often" interfere with work are far more likely to have sought treatment, suggesting crisis-point motivation rather than proactive care.
  • Employer benefits matter: Respondents at companies offering explicit mental health benefits show higher treatment rates — visible policy support lowers barriers to help-seeking.
  • Stigma suppresses disclosure: Fear of negative professional consequences for disclosing a mental health issue is prevalent and correlates with avoidance of treatment — workplace culture is a critical intervention point.
  • Geography reveals disparities: The US and Ireland lead in treatment-seeking rates; the choropleth highlights that access and cultural norms vary significantly across countries, pointing to systemic differences in mental healthcare adoption.
